Teaching

Business analytics, simulation, and data-driven decision making.

At National Tsing Hua University, I teach analytics as a decision-support discipline for uncertain business and service environments.

Current course

Business Analytics Using Simulation

Institute of Service Science, College of Technology Management, National Tsing Hua University

This course examines how managerial decisions perform under uncertainty in business systems that evolve over time. Through case-based simulations, students learn to frame decisions under incomplete information, evaluate uncertainty, diagnose performance outcomes, and communicate recommendations with managerial judgment.

Open Syllabus

Course focus

  • Simulation-based decision-making
  • Managerial decisions under uncertainty
  • AI-assisted tools for analytics workflows
  • Marketing and supply chain applications
  • Robustness, diagnosis, and recommendations

Modules

Case-based analytics across marketing and supply chain.

Module 1: Marketing

Students analyze marketing decisions involving demand creation, customer response, segmentation, experimentation, and product design.

  • Marketing response and budget allocation
  • Customer lifetime value and retention
  • Segmentation and targeting
  • Conjoint analysis and A/B testing
  • Marketing diagnosis and model limits

Module 2: Supply Chain

Students evaluate supply chain decisions involving fulfillment, capacity, timing, coordination, and resilience.

  • Inventory dynamics and replenishment
  • Demand forecasting and forecast error
  • Lead time, backlog, and stockouts
  • Scenario and robustness analysis
  • Bullwhip effect and capacity allocation

Learning outcomes

From model output to managerial judgment.

Students learn to frame managerial decisions under uncertainty, use simulation and AI-assisted tools as decision-support aids, diagnose performance outcomes, evaluate robustness, and communicate recommendations with practical judgment.
